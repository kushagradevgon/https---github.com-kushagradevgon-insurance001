Frequently Asked Questions

What is Life insurance?

Life insurance is an agreement between policyholder and insurance company, in which the insurance company agrees to pay a predetermined amount to the policyholder or their beneficiaries in the event of death, critical illness or personal disability (as laid out in their policies) in return for the payment of a premium amount.

What are tax benefits of life insurance?

Tax planning through investment in life insurance is widespread in India. Premiums paid up to a maximum of 1.5 lakh for life insurance policies are exempt under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961. Life insurance proceeds are not taxable for the deceased’s family under Section 10 (10D).
